# Founder Interview

This document keeps the skeptical cofounder questions and adds the founder's current answers from the product interview. The answers are not final forever, but they are the current operating view.

## Product Vision

### Questions

1. Are we building a career agent, a behavior-change program, a coaching product, a game, or a job-search tool?
2. What is the one sentence promise we would be embarrassed to break?
3. What should OpenLARP help users become that existing tools do not help them become?
4. Are we willing to be worse at resume editing than competitors if we are better at proof-building?
5. What will we explicitly refuse to build even if users ask for it?
6. What would make OpenLARP matter if LinkedIn, ChatGPT, Teal, and Handshake all copy daily quests?
7. Which part of the vision is essential, and which part is founder fantasy?
8. If OpenLARP is successful in five years, what behavior did it change in users?

### Current Answers

- OpenLARP starts as a daily career action program: "Duolingo for LinkedIn."
- The app should help users build proof, confidence, and job readiness.
- Proof is the foundation because confidence and readiness are weak if the user has nothing real to point to.
- The long-term vision is still a real career agent, but the first user experience should not feel like a chatbot.
- The brand can have "career hack" energy, but the hack must mean the fastest legitimate path, not deception.

## First User Segment

### Questions

1. Who is the first narrow user we can help better than anyone else?
2. Why would that user trust us before we have outcomes?
3. What exact moment causes this user to look for help?
4. Is their pain urgent enough to pay for, or only painful enough to talk about?
5. Are they underqualified, underconfident, poorly packaged, poorly networked, or poorly targeted?
6. Which of those problems are we actually prepared to solve first?
7. What user segment should we deliberately ignore for the first year?
8. Are college students and new grads the right first segment, or are they just the easiest to imagine?

### Current Answers

- Start with college students and new grads because the meme language fits them and the career anxiety is real.
- Everyone can use the product, but early positioning, UX, and growth should favor college and early-career users.
- The first product should focus on internships and entry-level jobs.

## Core Behavior

### Questions

1. What is the daily action users must take for OpenLARP to work?
2. What career actions do users avoid even when they know those actions matter?
3. What will we do when users refuse networking quests?
4. What will we do when users only want resume polishing?
5. What is the smallest quest that still creates real-world progress?
6. What makes a quest too small to matter?
7. What makes a quest too hard to complete?
8. How will we know whether a completed quest actually improved the user's odds?

### Current Answers

- The main loop is quest -> proof/self-report -> XP/streak/progress -> next quest.
- Users should usually see one main quest with a limited swap option.
- Difficulty should adapt: start easier, then increase as users show progress.
- V0 quests should focus on proof and prep.
- Skills, certificates, and deeper resource discovery belong to the later agent-search version.

## Retention

### Questions

1. Why would someone open OpenLARP tomorrow?
2. Why would someone open OpenLARP on day 7?
3. Why would someone still use OpenLARP after they get rejected?
4. Why would someone still use OpenLARP after they get an offer?
5. Are we trying to create daily retention, weekly retention, or episodic high-intent use?
6. What retention metric would convince us to build the app?
7. What retention metric would convince us to stop?
8. How will we distinguish healthy retention from guilt-driven retention?
9. What should happen after a user misses three days?
10. What should happen after a user completes a sprint?

### Current Answers

- The first product should use Today quests, a seven-day map, XP, badges, and forgiving streaks.
- Missed days can affect streaks, but the app should provide recovery quests instead of shame.
- The first major validation window is one month.
- The first-month bet is viral growth first, then subscription conversion.
- Subscription conversion after the free sprint is the main business proof, but growth is the first pressure test.

## Growth

### Questions

1. What would a user proudly share about OpenLARP?
2. What would a user be embarrassed to share?
3. Can "Am I Cooked?" grow without making users feel humiliated?
4. Which channel can produce the first 100 serious users?
5. Which channel can produce the first 1,000 retained users?
6. Are we building something people invite friends to, or something they use privately?
7. What proof would make a campus organization, creator, or community promote this?
8. What would make growth low quality?

### Current Answers

- The growth hook is "Am I Cooked?" on TikTok and Instagram.
- After the hook, explain the product as Duolingo for careers or Duolingo for LinkedIn.
- The first viral unit is a funny but hopeful cooked card.
- Later social proof should come from badges, streaks, and real user stories such as interviews or jobs.
- Use AI-tool creators, AI study/productivity creators, and campus creators.
- Creators apply through the website, provide past AI-promotion examples, and can receive beta access plus hook assets.
- Creator payment should be case by case.

## Monetization

### Questions

1. Who pays first: students, parents, schools, career switchers, creators, or employers?
2. What would users pay for before they get a job outcome?
3. Are users paying for advice, accountability, proof review, confidence, or outcomes?
4. What price creates commitment without exploiting anxiety?
5. Should V0 be paid, free, or paid after the diagnostic?
6. What refund promise is fair if users complete quests but do not get interviews?
7. Would schools buy this if Prentus and Handshake already exist?
8. Would employers trust a product with this brand?
9. What business model would corrupt the product's honesty?

### Current Answers

- The first 14-day sprint should be free.
- After the free sprint, users should convert into a monthly subscription.
- The target standard price is $29/month.
- There should be a student discount.
- The subscription should eventually unlock ongoing agent help, more content, and advanced search.

## AI Capabilities

### Questions

1. What must the AI do better than ChatGPT with a good prompt?
2. Which AI outputs require human review at the start?
3. Which AI outputs should never be shown without confidence checks?
4. What should the AI refuse to do?
5. How will we prevent fake precision in diagnostics?
6. How will we know whether AI recommendations are improving user behavior?
7. What data must be structured from day one so the AI can improve later?
8. What data should we avoid collecting even if it might improve recommendations?

### Current Answers

- No human should review user work in the product flow.
- V0 AI should power diagnostics, daily quests, proof quality checks, and progress summaries.
- Proof completion should combine AI quality checks and user self-report.
- Users start with a minimal profile; the app asks for more only when needed.
- AI can strongly frame real experience, but it cannot invent substantial facts.

## Agent Capabilities

### Questions

1. What is the difference between OpenLARP as a chatbot and OpenLARP as an agent?
2. What should the agent remember forever?
3. What should the agent forget automatically?
4. What should the agent be allowed to do without asking?
5. What should always require approval?
6. When should the agent challenge the user's goal?
7. When should the agent tell the user their target is unrealistic?
8. How proactive can the agent be before it feels invasive?
9. What agent mistake would destroy trust immediately?

### Current Answers

- OpenLARP should not open like a chatbot.
- It should open like a daily quest app.
- Chat should help users change goals, ask questions, or get unstuck.
- The agent should draft for approval, not act externally without approval.
- Long-term memory should keep important goals, proof, preferences, and outcomes.
- Sensitive chats should have a memory-off option that does not save them.
- Early job input should be pasted job text or links; direct integrations are a long-term goal.

## Notifications

### Questions

1. What notification would users thank us for sending?
2. What notification would make users uninstall?
3. Should reminders be daily, event-based, deadline-based, or user-chosen?
4. Should missed quests trigger encouragement, silence, or a recovery plan?
5. What is the maximum number of notifications per week before we become noise?
6. Should the product ever send urgent alerts?
7. What notification should be impossible for the system to send?
8. How will users control what the agent watches and when it interrupts them?

### Current Answers

- V0 should use push notifications in the app.
- Reminders should be useful nudges, not pure guilt.
- Proactive alerts should eventually include both quests/deadlines and opportunities.
- Users need control over what the agent watches and when it interrupts them.

## Community

### Questions

1. Does OpenLARP need community, or would community damage the private trust layer?
2. Should progress be private, pseudonymous, cohort-only, or public?
3. What would users actually want peers to see?
4. What would users never want peers to see?
5. Would peer accountability increase completion or shame?
6. What community norm would make OpenLARP defensible?
7. What community behavior would make the product toxic?
8. Should community exist before retention is proven one-on-one?

### Current Answers

- No community in V0.
- The product should be private by default.
- Users can choose to share cooked cards, streaks, badges, and outcomes.

## Onboarding

### Questions

1. What is the minimum information needed to give a useful first quest?
2. What question in onboarding would make users quit?
3. Should users upload a resume before getting value?
4. Should the first experience be a diagnostic, a quest, or a conversation?
5. How blunt should the first diagnosis be?
6. How do we make the user feel challenged without feeling judged?
7. What should happen if the user has no target role?
8. What should happen if the user has an unrealistic target role?

### Current Answers

- The first screen should start with goal setup.
- "Am I Cooked?" should happen after or during goal setup, not as the entire product.
- Users can skip some questions.
- If users complete onboarding, it can build a fuller profile.
- V0 should start with a minimal profile and deepen as needed.
- Tone should be meme-heavy at the hook, then blunt, kind, and professional when giving real help.
- "Am I Cooked?" should run at least once, recur weekly as a progress check, and trigger when setting a new goal.
- When goals change, ask whether the user wants to rebuild the path or adapt the current one.

## Daily Quests

### Questions

1. What percentage of quests should create an external artifact?
2. What percentage of quests should involve another person?
3. What should count as proof?
4. What should not count as proof?
5. Should users be allowed to swap quests?
6. Should users be allowed to choose easy mode?
7. How do we prevent quests from becoming busywork?
8. Who writes the first 100 quests?
9. What makes a quest uniquely OpenLARP rather than generic career advice?

### Current Answers

- V0 accepts text, links, photos, and screenshots as proof.
- Daily quests should be enough to show the user is making progress.
- XP should come from activity, proof, streaks, and outcomes, with major outcomes earning big bonuses or badges.
- The safest quest-choice model is one quest plus a limited swap option.
- The V0 progress view should emphasize goal readiness.

## Long-Term Engagement

### Questions

1. What does OpenLARP do after the first 14 days?
2. What does OpenLARP do after the user gets a job?
3. What does OpenLARP do when the user changes goals?
4. What does OpenLARP do when the user's life gets too busy?
5. What long-term memory would make the product hard to leave?
6. What long-term memory would feel creepy?
7. How do we keep advanced users from outgrowing the product?
8. How do we keep beginners from feeling permanently behind?
9. What is the long-term proof that OpenLARP makes users stronger candidates?
10. What evidence would make us shut down the current direction and start over?

### Current Answers

- After V0, add quest-linked resume help first.
- Then add interview prep.
- Agent search comes later because it requires cloud agent infrastructure.
- Agent search should default to courses, certificates, and projects, with filters for other categories.
- Android is ignored until there is meaningful traction.
