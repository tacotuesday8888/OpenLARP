# OpenLARP Executive Summary

## Core Verdict

OpenLARP is a promising idea, but not yet a defensible product.

The strongest part is the philosophy: "Fake it till you make it - honestly." That is emotionally sharp and timely. Many students, new grads, and career switchers feel behind, but they also know that fake credentials, AI-generated fluff, and mass applications are corrosive.

The weakest part is the feature list. Most listed features already exist across LinkedIn, Teal, Rezi, Careerflow, Huntr, Simplify, Handshake, Prentus, Jobscan, Final Round AI, AIApply, and general AI assistants.

OpenLARP should not start as a career toolbox. It should start as a daily proof-building system.

## Biggest Opportunities

### 1. Own The Private Transformation Layer

LinkedIn owns public professional identity. OpenLARP can own private preparation.

This is the strongest wedge:

> LinkedIn is where you show credibility. OpenLARP is where you build it.

### 2. Convert Anxiety Into Action

The target users are anxious, ambitious, and often stuck. They do not just need information. They need help doing the next uncomfortable thing.

OpenLARP can win if it makes one daily action feel possible.

### 3. Build Proof, Not Fluff

Most AI career tools improve packaging. OpenLARP can improve the underlying substance:

- Micro-projects.
- Networking proof.
- Interview stories.
- Target clarity.
- Skill evidence.
- Better applications.

### 4. Create A Memorable Emotional Hook

"Am I Cooked?" is a strong acquisition hook if handled carefully. It can drive curiosity and sharing.

But it must quickly shift from judgment to agency:

> "Here is how to become less cooked today."

### 5. Develop Longitudinal Personal Context

If OpenLARP tracks the user's goals, gaps, quests, proof, avoidance patterns, and outcomes over time, it can become more useful than a generic AI chat.

## Biggest Risks

### 1. No Current Moat

The visible features are easy to copy:

- XP.
- Levels.
- Daily quests.
- Resume help.
- LinkedIn help.
- Interview prep.
- Job tracking.
- Career plans.

The moat must come later from data, behavior, trust, proof graphs, retention, and distribution.

### 2. ChatGPT Substitution

Users can ask ChatGPT for career plans, resume edits, LinkedIn help, mock interviews, and job targeting.

OpenLARP must be more structured, more accountable, and more habit-forming than ChatGPT.

### 3. LinkedIn Platform Risk

LinkedIn can absorb many career features because it owns the profile, jobs, recruiter network, learning platform, and professional graph.

OpenLARP should not depend on being a LinkedIn optimizer.

### 4. Weak Daily Retention

Career apps are often episodic. Users return during job-search emergencies, not daily.

The daily quest loop must prove retention before building a native app.

### 5. Gamification Could Feel Childish

XP and levels may work for students but could repel professionals.

Gamification must support real progress, not replace it.

### 6. Ethical Brand Confusion

"Fake it till you make it" is risky. The "honestly" part must be enforced in product behavior.

The product must refuse to help users fabricate credentials or deceive interviewers.

## Most Likely Failure Modes

1. OpenLARP becomes an AI career chatbot with a cute skin.
2. Users try "Am I Cooked?" once and do not return.
3. Daily quests feel like homework.
4. Users prefer ChatGPT because it is more flexible.
5. Users prefer LinkedIn, Teal, or Huntr because they are closer to job applications.
6. The app rewards XP instead of real proof.
7. The product tries to serve students, switchers, professionals, and high schoolers at once.
8. The team builds SwiftUI/Firebase/RevenueCat before proving retention.
9. The brand attracts shortcut-seekers instead of honest improvers.
10. Competitors copy the visible product before OpenLARP has data or distribution.

## Recommended MVP

Build the "OpenLARP 14-Day Career Level-Up Sprint."

It should not start as a native app. It should start as a concierge-style MVP for college students and new grads targeting internships or entry-level jobs.

The MVP should include:

- Intake.
- "Am I Cooked?" diagnostic.
- One personalized 14-day questline.
- One daily quest.
- Proof submission.
- Gap-shrink progress.
- Weekly level-up report.
- Retention tracking.

Do not build:

- Xcode project.
- Firebase backend.
- RevenueCat subscription.
- Full resume builder.
- Full job tracker.
- Auto-apply.
- Interview copilot.
- Social network.

The MVP goal is to test whether users complete career-building actions daily.

## First 30 Days

### Days 1-3: Narrow The Segment

Choose one starting audience:

Recommended:

> College students and new grads targeting their first internship or entry-level role.

Define 3-5 target roles, such as:

- Product analyst.
- Data analyst.
- Marketing coordinator.
- Software engineering intern.
- UX/product design intern.

### Days 4-7: Build The Manual Sprint

Create:

- Landing page.
- Intake form.
- Diagnostic rubric.
- Quest template library.
- Progress report template.
- Manual user tracker.

Recruit 20-30 users manually.

### Days 8-21: Run The 14-Day Sprint

Deliver:

- Day 0 diagnostic.
- Daily quest.
- Proof review.
- Midpoint check-in.
- Weekly level-up report.
- Final report.

Measure:

- Day 1 completion.
- Day 3 return.
- Day 7 return.
- Day 14 completion.
- Proof quality.
- Repeat intent.
- Willingness to pay.

### Days 22-25: Interview Users

Ask:

- What made you return?
- What felt generic?
- What felt emotionally useful?
- What did you avoid?
- What would you pay for?
- What would you replace with ChatGPT?
- What did you do that you would not have done otherwise?

### Days 26-30: Decide

Proceed only if users show real behavior:

- They complete quests.
- They submit proof.
- They return without heavy founder chasing.
- They ask for another cycle.
- Some are willing to pay.

If the MVP passes, build a minimal iOS version focused only on:

- Onboarding.
- Diagnostic.
- Today quest.
- Proof submission.
- Progress.
- Weekly report.
- Notifications.

## Strategic Recommendation

Do not build the app yet.

Prove the loop first:

Current State -> Gap Analysis -> Personalized Plan -> Daily Quest -> Proof -> Progress -> Level Up.

If users will not complete this loop manually, a native app will not save it. If they do complete it, OpenLARP may have a real wedge: honest daily transformation for people who are ambitious but not yet credible enough for the opportunities they want.
